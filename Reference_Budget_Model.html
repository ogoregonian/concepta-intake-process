Budget & Service Bucket Model

How hours are allocated, tracked, and reported across service epics each billing cycle. Every ticket must be assigned to the correct service epic — this is the source of truth for client billing visibility and org-level resource planning.

← Executive Brief
💡
The ticket → epic → budget chain is the source of truth

Every activity logged to a Zoho ticket rolls up to a service epic. Every service epic is tracked against a pre-defined baseline hour allocation per billing cycle. This chain — Ticket → Epic → Budget → Insight — is what enables data-informed decisions for both the client and the Concepta team. Without clean, consistent epic assignment on every ticket, the entire budget visibility model breaks down.

1
Service Epics

The Five Service Buckets

All client accounts are structured around five service epics. Every ticket — whether a new request, production issue, maintenance task, or deployment — is assigned to one of these epics at intake. The epic assignment is made by the AI Agent at ticket creation based on the request type submitted.

Epic 1
Planning / Ops / New Requests
Covers all discovery, sprint planning, stakeholder calls, and new request analysis. New requests and enhancements are baked into this epic — reflecting the time required to analyze, scope, estimate, and route the request before any development begins. This is not a separate line item from new feature work.
Epic 2
Production Support
Covers all active production incidents, bug triage, hotfix implementation, SLA-bound emergency response, and post-release monitoring. Operates against defined office hours with a dedicated Dev + QA resource per account. SLA response times are defined per severity level (Sev 1–4) and agreed with client stakeholders before go-live.
Epic 3
Maintenance / Development
Covers approved feature development, enhancements, security and vulnerability remediation, dependency upgrades, and ongoing system maintenance. Work enters this epic only after passing through the intake and scoping process and receiving client scope approval. This is typically the largest budget bucket.
Epic 4
Deployments / Releases
Covers all staging preparation, QA coordination for release builds, production deployments, App Store submissions, post-release monitoring, and release documentation. App Store submissions must follow the agreed submission window (Monday–Wednesday) per account protocol.
Epic 5 — Safety Net
Buffer
A reserved allocation that absorbs unplanned ad hoc requests, coordination overhead, and overruns in any other service epic during the billing cycle. If any service bucket trends over its baseline, buffer hours are reallocated to it. The buffer utilization data informs the baseline reset conversation with the client at the next contract renewal. Clients must understand this mechanism — it is what enables continuous operation without disruption when unexpected work emerges.

Reference Allocation Model

Service Finance — 4-Week Cycle Baseline

The following allocation model is based on the Service Finance account operating on a 4-week billing cycle with a 519-hour total baseline. This serves as the reference model for structuring other accounts. Highlighted rows reflect buckets that require proactive client discussion.

Note: Bucket names in this model are standardized to match the epic naming convention used across all Concepta accounts and tooling.

Service Epic Purpose % of Baseline Hours (519 baseline) Notes
Planning / Ops / New Requests Discovery, sprint planning, stakeholder calls, new request analysis and scoping 10% 52 hrs New request analysis is baked into this epic — not a separate line item
Production Support Incidents, hotfixes, SLA-bound emergency triage and response 9% 45 hrs Dedicated Dev + QA with defined office hours per account
Maintenance / Development Approved feature development, enhancements, security work, maintenance 40% 207 hrs Largest bucket — monitor closely. Significant year-over-year increase requires client discussion
Deployments / Releases Staging, QA coordination, production deployments, App Store submissions 16% 83 hrs Increased from prior year — flag for client review at next contract cycle
Buffer Absorbs ad hoc requests and overruns; reallocated to overrun buckets as needed 25% 132 hrs Safety net — clients must understand this mechanism. Buffer utilization informs next-cycle baseline recalibration
📈
Year-Over-Year Observation — Use as a Conversation Starter

Total average monthly burn dropped from approximately 650–660 hours (2025) to 500–510 hours (2026), while the Maintenance / Development epic grew to 40% of the total mix. The 103% utilization observed in 2026 indicates buffer is being actively consumed — this pattern must be discussed with client stakeholders to determine whether baseline reallocation is appropriate for the next contract cycle. Data-informed baseline conversations remove opinion and bias from the discussion.

3
Per-Account Setup Requirements

What Must Be Defined Before Budget Tracking Can Begin

The following must be confirmed with client stakeholders before any tickets are tracked against service epics. Without this alignment, budget tracking is unreliable and the data-informed conversation model breaks down.

✓
Baseline Hours Per Service Epic
Starting hour allocations per service epic agreed with client stakeholders based on historical usage and anticipated workload. Reviewed and adjusted at each contract cycle based on observed burn rate data.
✓
Billing Cycle Cadence (3-week vs. 4-week)
Board open and close cadence confirmed per account. All time tracking must fall within the active board period. Board close dates must align with client billing dates.
✓
SLA Response Times by Epic and Severity
Acknowledgment and resolution SLAs defined for Production Support (Sev 1–4). Standard new request analysis and response targets defined for the Planning / Ops epic. All SLAs agreed with client stakeholders before go-live and loaded into the AI Agent's notification configuration.
✓
Production Support Office Hours and Resource Assignment
A dedicated Dev + QA resource assigned per account with defined monitoring windows for triage and incident response. Production support resources must not be double-booked against delivery or planning activities during their coverage windows.
✓
Budget Threshold Alert Level and Overage Escalation Path
The percentage of each service epic that triggers a proactive flag to the PDM — before the overage occurs — must be defined and loaded into the AI Agent. The stakeholder contact and approval process for any overage requiring sign-off must also be confirmed per account.
💡
The Buffer Epic is the Budget Safety Net — Clients Must Understand This

If any service epic trends over its baseline during a billing cycle, buffer hours are reallocated to it to maintain continuous operation. This is by design — it prevents work from stopping mid-cycle due to budget exhaustion. The buffer utilization data is then reviewed with the client at the next contract cycle to determine whether the baseline for that epic should be adjusted. Clients who understand this mechanism are more receptive to proactive budget conversations and less reactive when unexpected work emerges.
