Reference Document · Intake & Request Processing

Intake Request Form

Required and optional fields for both request types — the form the requestor submits is the routing mechanism. The AI Agent reads these fields to validate completeness, assign the correct billing epic, and route to the right workflow.

← Executive Brief
💡
Two forms. One entry point.

Two separate forms are available directly on the account's Basecamp Request Board. The requestor selects the appropriate form for their submission type. The required fields differ between the two forms — the AI Agent reads the submitted fields to validate completeness and confirm the request category. The PDM confirms accuracy during the oversight step. Neither the requestor nor the PDM manually labels the category — the form selection and field data determine routing.

New Request / Enhancement Form

Used when a requestor wants to introduce new functionality, enhance an existing feature, or request work that is not an active production issue.

Routes to: New Request workflow (Diagram 2) · Analyzing / Scoping / Estimating column · Planning/Ops/New Requests epic

Required fields: 4 (see below)

Reported Issue Form

Used when a requestor is reporting an active defect, incident, or production-impacting problem that requires triage and resolution.

Routes to: Production Issue workflow (Diagram 3) · Triage / Troubleshooting column · Production Support epic

Required fields: 10 (see below) — includes severity level assigned by requestor at submission

1
New Request / Enhancement — Required Fields

4 Fields Required Before Ticket Creation

All four fields must be present and complete before the AI Agent creates a ticket. If any are missing, the agent notifies the requestor and holds the submission in the Validate & Assign column until the gaps are resolved.

1
Request Title
A short, clear summary of the request. Used as the ticket title in Zoho and the card title on the Basecamp Request Board.
e.g. "Export capability for dealer reporting dashboard" · "Add MFA to borrower app login"
2
Problem Being Solved OR Goal Being Pursued (Most critical field)
The requestor must describe the underlying need — not the prescribed solution. The delivery team evaluates the best implementation approach. A solution-first description leads to scope creep and missed requirements.
Problem: "Dealers cannot export monthly reporting data needed for compliance reviews." / Goal: "Improve reporting visibility so dealers can monitor loan performance trends in real time."
3
Who Is Impacted?
The primary users or stakeholder groups affected by this request. Helps the team understand scope, testing requirements, and communication needs.
e.g. "All dealers" · "Internal finance team" · "All end users of the borrower app"
4
Where Does This Occur? (Platform or System)
The specific system, platform, or product where this request applies. Used to assign the correct development resources and confirm the implementation scope.
e.g. "SFC Dealer Mobile App" · "Mobile Back Office (Web)" · "SFC RN Mobile API" · "Admin portal"

Optional Fields Helpful but not required for ticket creation

5
Timeline or Deadline
Is this tied to a specific initiative, compliance requirement, event, or hard deadline? Helps the team understand urgency and prioritization weight.
e.g. "Required before the next dealer reporting cycle" · "M&A migration requires completion by November 2026"
6
Supporting Materials
Screenshots, screen recordings, error messages, mockups, or any documentation that helps explain the request or illustrates the desired outcome.
7
Business Impact if Not Addressed
What happens if this request is not resolved or deprioritized? Helps the team and stakeholders understand risk and make informed prioritization decisions.
e.g. "Compliance reporting will be delayed, risking audit findings" · "Client will lose approximately $X per day until resolved"
8
Additional Context
Any other information that helps the team better understand the request — history, constraints, related prior work, or known dependencies.
2
Reported Issue — Required Fields

10 Fields Required Before Ticket Creation

Production issues require more information at intake because the AI Agent uses the submitted data — particularly the severity level — to fire the correct SLA notification immediately upon receipt. All 10 fields must be present before the ticket is created. Missing fields trigger the same Validate & Assign hold state as new requests.

⚠️
Severity level is assigned by the requestor at submission — not by the team

Severity definitions (Sev 1–4) are agreed with client stakeholders per account before go-live and are available to the requestor directly on the intake form. The AI Agent reads the submitted severity level and fires the pre-configured SLA notification for that severity level immediately. If the Production Support team determines the severity is incorrect after review, they reassign it and the AI Agent updates the notification accordingly.

1
Issue Title
A short, clear summary of the issue. Used as the ticket title in Zoho and the card title on the Basecamp Request Board.
e.g. "Dealer app crashing on iOS 17 for iPad users" · "USPS API returning 500 errors on address validation"
2
Severity Level (Sev 1–4) (Assigned by requestor)
The severity level must be selected from the pre-agreed definitions for this account. Severity determines the SLA response time the AI Agent communicates to the requestor and governs the production support team's triage priority.
Sev 1: Critical — system down, no workaround · Sev 2: High — major function impaired · Sev 3: Medium — function degraded, workaround available · Sev 4: Low — minor issue, cosmetic
3
Problem Description
A clear description of what is happening and what the expected behavior should be. Describe the issue — not a proposed fix.
4
Steps to Reproduce
A numbered sequence of steps that consistently produce the issue. Without this, the production support team cannot reliably reproduce and diagnose the problem.
1. Open the dealer app on iOS · 2. Navigate to application submission · 3. Tap "Submit" — app crashes
5
Expected Behavior vs. Actual Behavior
What should happen vs. what is actually happening. This confirms the issue is a defect and not a misunderstanding of intended functionality.
6
Impacted Platform and Version
The specific application, platform, and version number where the issue is occurring. Required for the dev team to reproduce the issue in the correct environment.
e.g. "SFC Dealer App v3.10.3 · iOS 17.4 · iPhone 13" · "SFC Borrower App v2.3.0 · Android 14"
7
Impacted Components
Which features, modules, or integrations are affected by this issue. Helps the team scope the investigation and identify related dependencies.
e.g. "Application submission flow, USPS address validation API, iOS keyboard navigation"
8
Number of Users or Reports
How many users are experiencing this issue and over what timeframe. Helps the team assess severity accuracy and prioritization relative to other active issues.
e.g. "Reported by 12 dealers since the 2/9 release" · "Single report — unable to determine broader impact"
9
Console Logs or Error Messages (if available)
Any error codes, stack traces, or console output captured at the time of the issue. Significantly accelerates diagnosis for the development team.
10
Who Is Impacted?
The specific user group or stakeholder group experiencing this issue. Helps determine business impact, communication requirements, and escalation path.
e.g. "All dealers using the iOS app" · "Internal SFC QA team" · "Specific dealer accounts in the Southeast region"
