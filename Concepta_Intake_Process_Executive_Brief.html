Background & Current Challenges

Why This Initiative Exists

Concepta is a custom software development agency managing multiple client accounts simultaneously on a shared-resource model. As the organization scales, historically informal processes are creating measurable operational friction.

Challenge

Requests arrive through multiple informal channels — meetings, email, Basecamp, DMs, phone calls — with no single intake entry point.

Challenge

No standardized intake form or triage process leads to excessive time spent clarifying requests before work can even be evaluated.

Challenge

Incoming requests often lack the minimum information required for proper evaluation, estimation, or routing.

Challenge

Team members support multiple accounts simultaneously, making priority, timeline, and capacity visibility difficult to maintain.

Challenge

Operational workflows are not consistently documented, preventing teams from following a shared structure when processing work.

Challenge

Requests enter delivery pipelines without being assigned to the correct billing epic, creating budget tracking and reporting gaps.

Strategic Gap

Concepta is not currently utilizing AI agents within any of its agency software lifecycle operational processes — and the industry is moving fast. Agencies that integrate AI into their delivery operations now will have a compounding advantage in speed, consistency, and scalability. This initiative is the right starting point: the intake process is rule-based, repetitive, and high-volume — making it an ideal first use case to introduce an AI agent into our operational model. Getting into this game now, starting with a well-scoped MVP, positions Concepta to expand AI capabilities responsibly as the foundation matures.

💡
The Core Problem

Time, budget, and resource capacity are being lost to a reactive, lean operating model that was never designed to scale. We have been moving fast — but without the structure needed to deliver predictably. The result is ambiguity, rework, and client budgets spent on coordination overhead rather than meaningful outcomes. This initiative marks a deliberate shift: from reactive to predictable. To do this, we will need to revamp our operational model in partnership with both our internal team and our external account stakeholders — building a shared structure that gives clients clear visibility into where their budget is going, maximizes spend toward high-impact deliverables, and ensures work is delivered on time, on budget, and with quality as a baseline expectation — not an exception.

Goals

What We're Building Toward

Goal

Define a standardized intake and processing workflow across all accounts with clear entry points and routing rules.

Goal

Implement a structured triage and categorization process so requests are properly identified, labeled, and routed.

Goal

Ensure every request is assigned to the correct billing epic before entering the delivery pipeline.

Goal

Support predictable SLA response timelines by account and request type with defined severity handling.

Goal

Provide clients with structured outputs (scope, estimate, timeline) that enable informed prioritization decisions.

Data Goal

Drive decisions with data, not opinion. Every ticket logged against an epic. Every epic tied to a service budget. The chain — Ticket → Epic → Budget → Insight — is the source of truth for both client-facing reporting and internal resource planning.

Org Visibility Goal

Give Concepta the visibility to act before problems occur — resource capacity tracked in real time, budget burn trends per account, emerging workload spikes identified early, and maintenance budget baselines tracked cycle over cycle so the org can make proactive decisions on hiring, reallocation, and client conversations.

AI Agent Goal

POC an AI agent within the intake process — starting with request validation and ticket creation as the first scoped use case. Once validated, continue to refine the agent's capabilities within the intake workflow, then expand to other activities across the broader software delivery lifecycle as the foundation matures and confidence in the model grows.

Workflow Stages

The 5-Stage Intake Processing Workflow

All requests — regardless of type or channel of origin — move through these five stages before entering the delivery pipeline.

1
Stage 1

Request Intake Capture

The requestor submits a new request via the standardized intake form (Basecamp / Email). If received through informal channels (meeting, DM, phone call), the PDM converts it using the standardized intake fields. The completed intake is confirmed with the requestor before proceeding.

RequestorPDM (if conversion needed)
2
Stage 2

AI Agent: Validation, Ticket Creation & Notification

Once the requestor submits their form, the AI Agent picks up the submission and validates that all required fields for that request type are present and complete. The request category is confirmed by the form the requestor selected — New Request / Enhancement or Reported Issue — and the severity level for production issues is assigned by the requestor at submission based on pre-agreed definitions. If required fields are missing, the AI Agent notifies the requestor and holds the item in the Validate & Assign column until the gaps are resolved. Once validated, the agent creates the Zoho ticket, assigns the correct billing epic, moves the Basecamp card to the appropriate column, tags the assigned team in Slack, and notifies the requestor. Human oversight confirms ticket accuracy before the request advances.

AI AgentPDM (oversight)
📌
Prerequisites

Basecamp 4 active · Request Board configured · Zoho Sprint Backlog with "New Requests" and "Production Support" buckets · Billing epics with budget hours associated · Account Slack channel established · SLA definitions confirmed · Severity level definitions agreed per account

3
Stage 3

Internal Review, Triage & Workflow Routing

By the time the request reaches the internal team, the AI Agent has already validated the data, created the ticket, moved the Basecamp card to the correct column, and notified the requestor. The internal team's role at this stage is to confirm the AI Agent's handoff is accurate — verifying the ticket, epic, sprint bucket, and column assignment in Zoho and Basecamp. If the category is incorrect, the team reassigns it and the AI Agent updates the card and re-notifies the requestor. If the request is unclear (Loop 2), the team documents specific questions and coordinates with the requestor. Once confirmed, the request proceeds into the appropriate processing workflow based on the confirmed category.

💡
Production Issue workflow — additional steps in Stage 3

Production issues follow a dedicated workflow with additional checkpoints before a fix is implemented: budget check against remaining Prod Support allocation (with deployment budget and buffer as fallback); stakeholder escalation and sign-off if overage is required; RCA initiation for issues meeting the pre-agreed business impact criteria (runs alongside the fix, not as a gate); release version alignment (hotfix vs. scheduled release per severity and client agreement); fix implementation and QA validation in staging; production deployment; and requestor confirmation of resolution. All activity is logged in the Zoho ticket throughout.

📌
Prerequisites

New Request Processing Budget · Production Support budget with deployment budget and buffer allocation defined · Budget overage escalation path and stakeholder contacts identified · RCA criteria defined per account (severity level and business impact thresholds) · Release versioning approach agreed · Scheduled planning calls with necessary resources · Account SLA response definitions

4
Stage 4

Evaluation & High-Level Estimation

The delivery team assesses technical feasibility, identifies dependencies, considers implementation approaches, and develops a high-level level-of-effort estimate. This is not a detailed requirements phase — the goal is to produce enough information for the client to understand potential scope, effort, and impact.

PDMSADevQAUX/UI
📌
Prerequisites

DoD definition from team · New Request T-minus allocation · Scheduled planning calls · Tooling: Smartsheet Estimator, Notion, Figma, Mermaid/Lucid

5
Stage 5

Client Review, Approval & Prioritization

The Product Delivery team presents the client with a structured summary: confirmed problem or goal, high-level scope, implementation considerations, dependencies/risks, estimated range, and proposed timeline based on DoD. The client reviews and approves (or requests revisions) before the request advances into delivery planning.

Requestor / ClientPDM
💡
Only after client approval does work enter the delivery pipeline.

This gate ensures no development resources are committed until scope and budget alignment is confirmed.

Next Steps

Seven Actions. In This Order.

These are the actions required to move from framework to live operation. Each step enables the next — the sequence matters.

00

Working session cadence — established ✅

Tuesdays 4–5pm. Live working sessions focused on resource allocation and priorities — not written updates. This is the operating rhythm for everything below. Already confirmed between Raul and Anne.

01

Redefine account structure with each client

Re-align on service buckets, budget baselines, SLAs, severity definitions, schedules, and communication channels per account. Start with Service Finance as the reference model and work through all active accounts. This step unlocks everything else — nothing can be configured, tooled, or automated until account-level alignment is confirmed with client stakeholders.

02

Define and build the delivery toolkit

Configure the core delivery templates that support this framework per account — scope document template, Smartsheet Estimator (covering all activities required to achieve DoD), Smartsheet Project Plan template (initiated at start of analysis), and RCA documentation template. All templates must be validated per account before any work moves through the process.

03

Align on planning and ops ceremonies

Define the full ceremony schedule per account — cadence, attendees, and committed resources. Fold new request processing into those ceremonies so intake analysis happens within the established planning rhythm. Lock resource allocation across accounts to prevent overlap and double-booking of shared resources.

04

Pilot on the corporate site

Use the corporate site project as a controlled pilot — the PDM team acts as both the client (submitting requests) and the delivery team (processing them). Run the full intake workflow end to end: submit a request, validate it, create the ticket, move the card, route it to the correct workflow, process it through analysis or triage. Find every gap, validate the model, and prove it works before it touches a live client account. This is the most important step before broader rollout.

💡
Why the corporate site?

Zero client risk. Full team control. Real work, real process, real gaps surfaced in a safe environment. The learnings from this pilot directly inform the per-account rollout sequence and the AI Agent configuration.

05

Review and align on all three workflow diagrams

Walk the full PDM team through Diagrams 1, 2, and 3 together. Confirm the processes are correct, every role and handoff is understood, and the team is aligned before go-live. Diagrams must reflect the process as it will actually be operated — not aspirationally. Any gaps identified during the pilot should be incorporated before this review.

06

Internal labs: configure AI Agent MVP

Only after the manual process has been validated through the corporate site pilot. Configure Zoho, Basecamp, and Slack integrations per the AI Agent Per-Account Configuration Requirements (Section 9). Define validation and routing logic, set up notification templates, configure monitoring trigger thresholds (inactivity windows, budget alert levels, SLA cadences), and run a full end-to-end POC against one pilot account before broader rollout. The internal labs team and PDM are jointly responsible for this step.
